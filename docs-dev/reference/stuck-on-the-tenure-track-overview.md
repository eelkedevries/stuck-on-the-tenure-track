# Stuck on the Tenure Track

*A satirical browser game about academic life*

---

## What this is

*Stuck on the Tenure Track* is a turn-based simulation of an academic career, from undergraduate matriculation to the tenure decision. You and three of your former high-school classmates have left school with the same delusional ambition: to be the first of your cohort offered tenure. One of you will make it. The others will finish second, third, or quietly leave for industry, where they will be happier and earn more.

The game is satire, but the mechanics are drawn from real academic life. The aim is recognition over fantasy: you should laugh because the game is accurate, not because it is absurd. If you have ever opened an email from a reviewer and felt your shoulders rise toward your ears, this game is for you.

The beta covers psychology, with four sub-disciplines: cognitive, social, clinical, developmental.

---

## How it plays

Each turn represents one academic term, about three months of in-game time. You allocate finite time across competing demands and live with the consequences. A full game is roughly twenty-five turns and takes about thirty minutes to play, in any modern browser, on a smartphone or a computer.

**Research.** Run studies, write papers, submit to journals, survive peer review. Each paper is tracked individually, with its own co-authors, journal, methodology, and citation trajectory. Where you publish and with whom matters; so does what you put your name on.

**Teaching and service.** Real demands on real hours. Neglect them and the department notices. Over-invest and your research suffers.

**Funding.** Grant calls open and close on the academic calendar. Applications cost weeks of writing. Most fail. The ones that succeed transform your career.

**Relationships.** Your partner, your family, your friends, your collaborators, and your supervisor are tracked separately. Each decays at its own rate when neglected. Some leave. Some stop returning your emails. Your PhD supervisor is the single most consequential person in the early game, and you do not get to choose them as carefully as you would like.

**Health.** Sleep debt, mood, and physical health are coupled. Burnout, anxiety, and depression are not flavour text — they impose real penalties and require real time to manage. Some conditions become chronic. The game treats this honestly.

**Specialisation.** You begin in psychology without a sub-discipline. Through coursework, MSc choice, and supervisor selection, you specialise progressively into cognitive, social, clinical, or developmental psychology. The choice shapes which journals you publish in, which grants you can apply for, and which scandals you live through.

---

## Milestones

Four explicit milestones structure the career. Each can be failed, with consequences:

- Successful defence of your MSc thesis
- Successful defence of your PhD dissertation
- Appointment as assistant professor
- The tenure decision

The tenure decision is the win condition. The committee weighs publications, citations, grants, teaching, and service — and is, in any given case, partly stochastic. Strong candidates are sometimes rejected. Weak ones are occasionally promoted. The game does not explain why.

---

## Misconduct

Research misconduct is in the game because it is in academia. You can engage in grey-area practices with low per-instance detection risk that compounds over time. You can fabricate data, with higher risk and worse consequences. Your students and RAs can do it on your watch, and the responsibility is partly yours.

When detection happens, it is unceremonious. *Retraction Watch* posts your paper. Your h-index drops. Your collaborators stop replying. The game does not lecture. It does not need to.

---

## The ending

Every game ends with a CV screen formatted in the conventions of your field: publications, citations, h-index, grants, positions held. Beside it, in smaller print, what was given up — relationships ended, hobbies abandoned, weeks on sick leave, chronic conditions acquired, cities lived in for less than two years.

You can save the CV. You can share it. You cannot edit the second list.

---

## Platforms, length, scope

- Plays in any modern browser, on smartphone or computer.
- One full game takes around thirty minutes.
- Save-and-resume on every turn.
- Single-player against three computer rivals at beta.
- Multiplayer, additional broad disciplines, and additional jurisdictions are deferred.
